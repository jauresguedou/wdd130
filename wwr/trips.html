South Fork American River Rafting Gorge

Day 1- 14 miles

Overview

Rafts push off into the lower South Fork, easing into the river with a series of fun Class II riffles that let paddlers find their stroke. Within the first few river miles, the crew navigates Barking Dog rapid followed by Fowler’s Rock, giving everyone a taste of whitewater under the watchful eye of experienced guides

Middle Fork of the Salmon River

Day 5:Explore side hikes & wildlife

Overview

The afternoon stretches bring some of the trip’s most challenging rapids. Expect steep, technical Class IV chutes like Devil’s Tooth and Green Dragon, where precise timing and teamwork keep you out of the powerful holes below. Between boisterous waves, scan the granite walls for bighorn sheep perched on ledges and keep an eye skyward for peregrine falcons diving along the cliff faces.

Illinois River (Oregon)

Days 2–3: Tackle major rapids by day, explore side-canyon hot springs and short hikes, then camp riverside each night

Overview

Numerous feeder creeks carve the canyon walls into mini-gorges. At moderate spring flows (600–1,200 cfs), guides paddle small rafts or packrafts a short distance into side canyons, then lead 10–15 minute hikes up moss-lined trails to hidden waterfalls
